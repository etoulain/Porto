The classic way to see Alfama's narrow, steep streets without the uphill walk with a 5yo. Touristy, but genuinely the easiest way through. Board early — it fills up fast.

Grand riverside square in Baixa, yellow arcaded buildings opening right onto the Tagus. Climb the Arco da Rua Augusta for a rooftop view over the square and river.

Douro river dinner cruise options
Which cruise to book
  • Cruzeiros Douro — "6 Bridges + WOW Dinner" — most family-friendly: ~50 min cruise + sit-down dinner at WOW. Has a kids' menu (4–12).
  • Tomaz do Douro / Living Tours — 3-hour evening cruise from Ribeira dock (8pm), 3-course dinner, live music. Adult evening-out vibe.
  • Sailing 360º — "Dinner on Board" — smaller boat, 3-hour dinner cruise, good for families, catches the sunset Jun–Oct.
  • Avoid for a family evening: "Douro Party Boat", Living Cruise's sunset cruise — DJ / bachelor-party boats, smoking on board.

Practical notes
  • Car: Porto's centro has narrow streets and pricey parking — use a garage near Bolhão rather than the street. Cascais and Gaia are easier.
  • Dog: Jardins do Palácio de Cristal, Parque da Cidade, Parque Marechal Carmona and Quinta do Pisão are your best relief/run spots.
  • Indoor venues: Palácio da Bolsa, Jerónimos Monastery, Livraria Lello, World of Discoveries, SEA LIFE, the wine cellars and the Douro cruise likely don't allow dogs — decide in advance who splits off.
  • Pharmacies: look for the green cross; ask which one is "de serviço" (on duty) after hours.
  • Both flights: Air France requires staffed counter check-in with a pet in the cabin — don't rely on kiosk timing.
